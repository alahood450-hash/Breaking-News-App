01 — Overview

THE APP
AT A GLANCE

Breaking News is a mobile-first news platform designed for readers who demand speed and clarity. The app surfaces live stories, categorized feeds, and personalized saved articles — all within a bold black-and-red visual identity.

The project included two distinct user modes: a Reader who consumes and saves news, and a Journalist who can publish directly to the platform. This dual-role system required thoughtful information architecture across every screen.

The design challenge was to create urgency through UI without causing fatigue — making the critical feel critical, while keeping the reading experience comfortable.

03 — Challenge

PROBLEM
& SOLUTION

What we found

The Problem

  • News apps use generic, interchangeable designs — no visual urgency for breaking events
  • Readers have no quick way to distinguish live stories from older content in a scrolling feed
  • Bookmark systems are buried, reducing return engagement significantly
  • No dual-role system — journalists and readers use completely separate apps or awkward admin panels
  • Category navigation requires too many taps to reach specific topic feeds
How we responded

The Solution

  • Bold red accent system signals urgency — LIVE badges, category tags, and accent bars create visual hierarchy instantly
  • Horizontal scroll carousel at the top of Home surfaces live stories above the main feed
  • Persistent bookmark icon on every card makes saving a one-tap action from any screen
  • Journalist/Reader toggle in Profile — one app, two modes, zero context switching
  • Category grid with prominent red buttons for fast one-tap navigation to any topic
04 — Process

DESIGN
PROCESS

01
Research
The research phase involved reviewing existing mobile news applications and UI patterns to understand how news content is typically presented. This exploration helped identify opportunities to improve the visibility of breaking news, navigation between categories, and quick access to saved articles — guiding the early wireframes and overall information architecture.
02
Define
Personas — Reader: Browses breaking news, saves articles. — Journalist: Publishes news, follows live updates. User Journey Reader → views live news → browses categories → saves articles. Journalist → creates article → publishes → monitors engagement. Pain Points — Hard to spot live news — Navigation takes multiple steps — Publishing workflow can be unclear
03
Design
3 rounds of wireframing. Developed the black-and-red visual system. Built 46 unique screens across Home, Categories, Saved, Article, Profile, and Post News.
04
Test
The design was reviewed and refined through self-evaluation and design reviews. Several improvements were made to the Reader/Journalist toggle system, the news card layout, and the visual hierarchy of breaking news. These adjustments were implemented through multiple design iterations to improve overall usability.
